Education

Ridgewood High School (2012-2016)

University of Maryland (2016-)

  • September 2012

    From Humble Beginnings

    In September 2012, at the age of fourteen I enrolled in Ridgewood High School in my hometown of Ridgewood, New Jersey. Although I did not have much actual experience or knowledge in computer science, computing, or programming, this was the year my interest in CS began to develop. I did not take any formal CS classes freshman year, I spent most of my downtime learning basic web design (HTML & CSS) and building a website.

  • September 2013

    First CS Class

    The next year in September 2013, I took my first actual high-level computer science class taught at RHS: an honors introductory course to programming in Visual Basic .NET. It was my first real experience developing computer programs, though I did have previous knowldge writing Java code going into the class, as I had been learning it online.

  • January 2014

    Intro to Java H

    In January of the new year for my second semester class of sophomore year, I took my second computer science honors course: Introduction to Java Programming. Although I had self-taught myself the beginner skills and concepts of the Java programming language, this class built on those ideas and gave me real experience writing Java programs.

  • September 2014

    Computer Science AP

    In the Fall of my junior year, I took Computer Science AP, a substantial step-up from my previous CS courses. I remember being particularly excited for this class because by this point my high school career I knew computer science was my calling. During this interesting year-long course, I continued to improve my skill and knowledge in writing Java code, object-oriented programming, and computing.

  • September 2015

    Advanced Topics in CS H

    With a budding knowledge of Java and object-oriented programming, I took a year-long honors course above CS AP offerred at my high school: Advanced Topics in CS. The course covered a broad array of computer science topics including learning various programming languages (Java, C++, C, & Swift), building and using data structures, and application development (iOS & Android), all in only the first semester of my senior year.

  • January 2016

    Second Semester Senior

    With the turn of the new year came new research topics in my CS class and new experiences. From creating C++ programs and learning about data structures in the first semester, we moved on to iOS and Android application development and PHP scripting for a webserver with a MySQL backend to streamline our school's official application (RHS App). I was also accepted to the College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences (CMNS) at the University of Maryland in late January.

  • August 2016

    University of Maryland

    On August 29th, I began my first semester at the University of Maryland, College Park. After having visited UMD three times (two campus visits and my Freshman Orientation), I was eager to start my freshman year and take my first college classes. This semester I am taking Object-Oriented Programming I, Calculus I, Academic Writing, and Special Topics in Gov't & Politics: Religions, Beliefs, & World Affairs. Although only two of them relate to my intended major of Computer Science, they are all interesting and practical classes. Can't wait to see what new knowledge and skills I will acquire, whether it be in terms of the realm of computing or in general.

  • January 2017

    Second Semester

    After the turn of the new year and a lengthy and much-needed Winter Break, I returned to UMD on January 25th to begin my second semester of college. I was enthustiastic about further developing my computer science knowledge, through my coursework, personal projects, and the various computing events (like hackathons and club meetings). This semester I am taking Object-Oriented Programming II, an extension on last semester's course, to further my extensive knowledge in Java and OOP principles, as well as implement a large variety of data structures. In addition, I am taking Calculus II, Discovering New Ventures, Oral Communication, and Introductory Linguistics. I also began developing a multiplatform 2D game in C# using the Unity game engine, Blocky, which is currently in the beta stages of development, during a 36-hour hackathon here at Maryland (Bitcamp 2017).

  • June 2017

    Summer Internship at MDCO

    On June 5th, I began working as a summer intern in the Information Technology department, specifically on the Digital Solutions team, at The Medicines Company in Parsippany, NJ. MDCO is a biopharmeutical company, whose mission is to create transformational digital solutions to address the most pressing healthcare needs facing patients, physicians, and providers. In other words, the IT department and Digital Solutions teams work to create innovative software to aid companies and our partners, primarily web and mobile applications. As one of very few interns in the department, I assist in any way I can, such as data entry, documentation, programming, software testing, and technical support for various projects. One of my main tasks is to provide analytics for one of our newly-released web applications, The ORION Project, using Microsoft Azure developer tools such as Application Insights and Power BI.

  • August 2017

    Sophomore Year: CS, AR, DU

    After a productive, hard-working, and fun summer away from campus, I was excited to return for a tough, but important upcoming semester. In fall 2017, I began my first semester of sophomore year, which was my first semester taking three major courses (two for computer science, one for mathematics). My rather rigorous schedule consisted of Introduction to Computer Systems, Discrete Structures, and Calculus III, along with Entrepreneurial Opportunity Analysis and Decision-Making in 21st Century Technology Ventures, and Writing in the Digital World; the latter two were to satisfy my minor and general education requirements, respectively. In addition to my academic commitments this fall, I began assisting the Augmented Reality Club as a front-end web developer, which has been a fun, yet challenging learning experience; here is a link to the progress and documentation site. Another highlight of my fall semester was joining a social fraternity, in fact it is the sixth-oldest founded in North America, Delta Upsilon (DU). I was elected to the eight-man Executive Board of the Maryland Chapter of DU, as Vice President of Membership Education. Overall, continuing my studies in computer science, working as a member of the AR web development team, and joining and running for the Executive Board of the Delta Upsilon Fraternity at UMD were my favorite parts of the fall semester.
